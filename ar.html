A. R. Rahman

Indian musical composer and record producer

The Mozart of Madras

A.R. Rahman is a renowned Indian composer, singer, songwriter, musician, and philanthropist. He was born A.S. Dileep Kumar on January 6, 1966, in Madras (now Chennai), India. Rahman's father, R.K. Sekhar, was a prominent Tamil musician who composed scores for the Malayalam film industry. Rahman began studying piano at the age of four and developed an interest in electronics and computers. After his father's death when Rahman was nine years old, he began playing piano professionally to support his family. He dropped out of school but later received a scholarship to study at Trinity College, Oxford, where he earned a degree in Western classical music. Rahman's music career spans over three decades, and he has composed music for over 100 films. He is known for his work in films like Roja (1992), Lagaan (2001), and Slumdog Millionaire (2008), among many others. He has won numerous awards for his music, including six National Film Awards, fifteen Filmfare Awards, and sixteen Filmfare Awards South. Rahman is also known for his philanthropic work, particularly in the field of education. He has performed at several charity concerts and has supported organizations that work towards providing quality education to underprivileged children. In addition to his music and philanthropic work, Rahman has also been recognized for his contributions to global music. He has received awards from Stanford University and has been featured in the Limca Book of Records as "Indian of the Year for Contribution to Popular Music"..
